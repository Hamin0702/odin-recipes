Description

Tsukemen is a style of Japanese ramen where the noodles and broth are served separately. Diners dip cold or room-temperature noodles into a rich, concentrated hot broth before eating. It's refreshing, flavorful, and offers a fun twist on traditional ramen.

üìù Ingredients

Noodles:

  • 2 servings thick ramen noodles (fresh or frozen)
  • Cold water and ice for rinsing

Dipping Broth:

  • 2 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp dashi powder (optional, for extra umami)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1/2 tsp chili oil (optional, for spice)

Toppings (optional but recommended):

  • Chashu (braised pork belly)
  • Menma (seasoned bamboo shoots)
  • Soft-boiled egg (ajitsuke tamago)
  • Chopped green onions
  • Nori (seaweed)
  • Toasted sesame seeds

üç≥ Steps

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil ramen noodles according to package instructions. Rinse under cold water, then plunge into ice water to chill. Drain well and set aside.
  2. Prepare the broth: In a saucepan, combine chicken or pork broth with soy sauce, mirin, vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, ginger, and optional dashi. Simmer gently for 5‚Äì10 minutes.
  3. Add heat and depth: Stir in chili oil (if using) and adjust seasoning to taste. Keep the broth hot for dipping.
  4. Prepare toppings: Slice chashu, boil eggs to soft-center, and gather other toppings as desired.
  5. Assemble and serve: Place chilled noodles in a bowl. Serve broth separately in a smaller bowl. Arrange toppings on the noodles or on the side. Dip noodles into the broth and enjoy!
